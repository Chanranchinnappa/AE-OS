Affiliate Disclosure

This page explains how Brightpaths does — and does not — make money, in plain language, because disclosure buried in fine print is not disclosure.

Current status

As of today, Brightpaths contains no affiliate links and earns no revenue of any kind. Every product-related statement on this site was written without payment, sponsorship, or free samples from any manufacturer or retailer.

If affiliate links are added

The site may in future join affiliate programs (such as Amazon Associates). If that happens, three commitments apply from day one. First, this page will be updated to name the specific programs, including any identification statement those programs require. Second, pages containing affiliate links will say so clearly near the links themselves — not only here — consistent with the FTC's endorsement guidance. Third, the nature of the arrangement will be stated plainly: the site earns a commission on qualifying purchases made through such links, at no additional cost to you.

What commissions will never do

Commissions will never reorder a recommendation. The site's buying advice is built from published specifications and the mechanics of how the hardware works — battery replaceability, panel quality, charge control — as laid out in our buyer's guide. If a product with a worse commission is the better fixture, the better fixture wins. A recommendation that can be bought is worth nothing to the reader, and readers are the only audience this site has.

Sponsored content

Brightpaths currently publishes no sponsored content. If that ever changes, sponsored pieces will be labeled as such at the top of the page, not disguised as independent guides.

Why disclose this much

Affiliate arrangements are legal and common; hiding them is what erodes trust. The point of stating all of this before a single affiliate link exists is that the policy gets written while there is no money on the table to bend it. When links do arrive, you will be able to compare the site's behavior against commitments that predate its first commission — which is exactly the test an independent guide should be able to pass.

Questions

Anything unclear about the above can be raised through the site's public GitHub repository. Data-collection practices are covered separately in the privacy policy.
