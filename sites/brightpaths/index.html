Practical Guides to Solar Garden Lighting

Brightpaths is a small, independent site with one job: explain solar garden and path lighting clearly enough that you can buy once and buy right. No sponsored rankings, no recycled press releases — just plainly written guides grounded in how the hardware actually behaves outdoors.

Solar lighting is one of the few home upgrades that is genuinely self-contained: no trenching, no electrician, no running cost. But it is also a category full of near-identical products and vague claims, where the difference between a light that survives three winters and one that dies by February rarely appears on the box. That gap — between what the packaging says and what the fixture does — is what this site exists to close.

Start here

If you are new to solar lighting, read how solar garden lights work first. It covers the four components every fixture shares — panel, battery, LED, and controller — and why the battery is nearly always the part that decides how good a light really is.

Wondering whether solar lights are worth it at all? See how long solar lights last, which separates the lifespan of the fixture from the lifespan of the battery inside it, with sourced figures rather than guesses.

Ready to buy? The solar path light buyer's guide walks through the six specifications that matter, the ones that do not, and the red flags that reliably predict disappointment.

How this site is written

Every factual claim on Brightpaths either cites a public source or is stated as practical guidance from the way the components work. Where we do not have a source, we say so instead of inventing a number. Pages are updated when the underlying facts change, and each update is logged.

What you will not find here: sponsored placements dressed up as reviews, affiliate rankings reordered by commission, or lifespan claims copied from a manufacturer's box. Where we eventually recommend specific products, the criteria behind each pick will be spelled out on the page, so you can apply the same checklist to any fixture we have not covered.
