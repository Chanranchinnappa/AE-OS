Privacy Policy

Brightpaths is a static informational website. The honest summary of this policy is that the site collects as close to nothing as a website can: there are no user accounts, no comment forms, no newsletter, no advertising tags, no analytics scripts, and the site sets no cookies. Reading these pages does not require you to hand over anything, and there is no mechanism here that would accept your data if you tried.

What the site itself collects

Nothing. The pages are plain HTML and CSS. They contain no tracking pixels, no third-party embeds, no fingerprinting scripts, and no forms. Your reading habits on this site are not recorded by this site.

What the hosting provider may collect

Brightpaths is served by GitHub Pages. Like effectively all web hosts, GitHub may collect standard server logs — such as visitor IP addresses — for security and operational purposes. That collection is governed by GitHub's privacy statement, not by this site, and we do not receive or review those logs.

External links

Guides on this site link to external resources (for example, the U.S. Department of Energy). Once you follow a link away from Brightpaths, the destination site's own privacy practices apply. We link to sources because citation matters, not because we have any relationship with their operators.

If this ever changes

If the site later adds analytics, advertising, or affiliate links, this policy will be updated before those additions go live, and any tracking they involve will be disclosed here plainly. The site's operating principle is to remain cookieless wherever possible. Monetization plans and their disclosure rules are described in the affiliate disclosure.

Your rights

Because this site holds no personal data about you, there is nothing for you to request, correct, or delete here — access and erasure rights under laws like the GDPR would apply to data we simply do not have. If hosting logs concern you, the request path is GitHub's privacy contact, since those logs live with them, not with us. We consider not collecting data in the first place a stronger protection than any deletion process.
